Sustainable Development Goals

The sustainable development goals (SDGs) are a set of 17 global goals adopted by the United Nations in 2015 to end poverty, protect the planet, and ensure peace and prosperity for all by 2030. Each goal has specific targets and indicators to measure progress and achievements. The SDGs are interlinked and interdependent, meaning that the success of one goal depends on the success of others.

Transport is a key sector that contributes to the achievement of many SDGs, especially those related to climate change, energy, cities, health, and economic growth. By shifting to green transport, which is a way of travelling that reduces environmental impact, saves money, and improves health, we can support the following SDGs:


SDG 3: Good health and well-being. Green transport can reduce air pollution, noise pollution, and traffic congestion, which can cause respiratory diseases, cardiovascular diseases, stress, and accidents. Green transport can also increase physical activity, reduce obesity, and improve mental health, by promoting modes such as cycling and walking.


SDG 7: Affordable and clean energy. Green transport can reduce the dependence on fossil fuels, which are the main source of greenhouse gas emissions and air pollution. Green transport can also increase the use of renewable energy sources, such as solar, wind, and hydro, which are cleaner and cheaper than fossil fuels.

SDG 9: Industry, innovation and infrastructure. Green transport can foster innovation and infrastructure development, by creating new technologies, products, and services that are more efficient and sustainable. Green transport can also enhance the quality and accessibility of transport infrastructure, such as roads, railways, ports, and airports, which are essential for economic growth and social inclusion.

SDG 11: Sustainable cities and communities. Green transport can make cities and communities more livable, resilient, and inclusive, by improving urban mobility, reducing urban sprawl, and enhancing urban design. Green transport can also support the provision of basic services, such as education, health, and water, by improving access and affordability.

SDG 13: Climate action. Green transport can mitigate the impacts of climate change, by reducing greenhouse gas emissions and improving energy efficiency. Green transport can also adapt to the effects of climate change, by increasing the resilience and flexibility of transport systems and services.

  • These are some of the SDGs that can be achieved if we shift to green transport.     Why choose green transport?

    Green transport is a way of travelling that reduces your environmental impact, saves you money, and improves your health. Here are some benefits of choosing green transport options:

    • Reduce greenhouse gas emissions: By using public transit, cycling, walking, or carpooling, you can avoid burning fossil fuels and emitting harmful gases that contribute to global warming.
    • Save money: By using green transport, you can save on fuel costs, parking fees, tolls, and vehicle maintenance. You can also take advantage of discounts, subsidies, and incentives offered by some governments and organizations for using eco-friendly modes of transportation.
    • Improve your health: By using green transport, you can increase your physical activity, reduce your stress levels, and improve your mood. You can also avoid exposure to air pollution, noise pollution, and traffic congestion that can harm your health.
